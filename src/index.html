In this game you will be presented with a sequence of numbers, one by one. The numbers are generated randomly from a hidden concept. The goal is to guess the hidden concept earlier than the computer, which uses Bayes' Theorem to calculate the probability of the hidden concept. In every turn, you are given a number generated by the hidden concept and you can choose between two options: 1. If you feel as if you don't have enough information to infer the hidden concept, you can you can pass by pressing the "pass" button. 2. If you want to guess the hidden concept, you can enter the generating formula into the textfield. Formulas are written with the use of a variable "z" and the formula is then evaluated with all relevant integers. For example, "1+ 2z" generates all odd numbers, "3+4z" generates 3 plus all multiple of 4 and "z" generates all numbers. Simply inputting the formula lets you visualize your current guess. If you are confident about the hidden concept, you can hit the "Guess" button to submit your guess. If your guess is correct, you get one point. The first player to reach 10 points wins the game. Notice, that numbers are only given in the interval [0, 100] and concepts are only part of the concept collection, if the concept generates 5 or more numbers in this interval. Enjoy!
